The Indian Ocean Trade route was one of the most impactful trade routes of the medieval times. The three main reasons for this claim are that the Indian Ocean Trade route made trading faster, connected many countries, and brought wealth to many different kingdoms and empires.
The Indian Ocean Trade route was called the "Monsoons Marketplace" for a reason. If you didn't get the hint, it was because of the monsoon winds. This actually stimulated the popularity of the Indian Ocean Trade route. This is because the monsoon winds actually made trade faster. Traders would savor this so much because the monsoon winds were also extremely predictable. This made it particularly easy for the trader to plan trips according to their destination. For example, from October to November, cool, dry air is drawn from the Himalayas and blown southeast across the Arabian Sea towards Africa. This actually allowed merchants to make a round trip to India possibly within an year. In contrast, doing the same trip taking the Silk Road actually took more than an year, despite the distance advantage. Boat travel was most commonly used in the Indian Ocean Trade route. Trade benefited from this in general, but the ocean currents and the monsoon winds created a jolting duo in terms of speed. These factors led the Indian Ocean Trade route to have its well known speed.


Almost all trade routes connected countries, but not to the extent that the Indian Ocean Trade route took it to. The Indian Ocean Trade route connected the vast empires and kingdoms along Southeast Asia, India, Arabia, and East Africa. This had many benefits such as diverse religions and materials being traded. For example, China and Africa were able rise in the gold industry, giving many people access to gold through the Indian Ocean Trade route. Iron works also thrived, giving many different countries awareness to different materials and new crafting styles. These are just a few examples of the possible sight of encoutners (SOE). Religion also had an opportunity to spread. While other trade routes connected countries that possesed similar religions, the Indian Ocean Trade route connected countries with many different religions. For example, Buddhism and many other Chinese influences spread along the Indian Ocean Trade route. Spice trade was a result of the spread of culture. Overall, the Indian Ocean Trade route linked many different countries and made it possible to create new SOE's.


The previous paragraph mentioned how iron and gold works could be traded through the Indian Ocean Trade route. Well believe it or not, the Indian Ocean Trade route actually made other countries wealthy. I know this sounds like one of those sketchy "How to become rich ads," but it's not. The Indian Ocean Trade route made the booming trade of gold possible by creating many connections to gold wielders such as Africa. West Africans extensively mined gold, which was eventually used to make Almoravid Gold Coins. This currency became popular in north-west Africa, as well as the Iberian Peninsula. The once humble Almoravid Gold Coin became popular in those areas, finally influencing more currencies to emerge. This influenced new types of money like paper money in China. Thus, trading with money started to increase, giving many countries the opportunity to become wealthy.
