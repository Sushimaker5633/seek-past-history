Indian Ocean Trade
The Indian Ocean Trade route was one of the most impactful traderoutes of the medieval times. The three main reasons for this claim is that the Indian Ocean Trade route made trading faster, it connected many countries, and it also brought wealth to many different kingdoms and empires.
The Indian Ocean Trade route was called the "Monsoons Marketplace" for a reason. If you didn't get the hint, it was because of the monsoon winds. This actually stimulated the popularity of the Indian Ocean Trade route. This is because the monsoon winds actually made trade faster. Traders would savor this so much because the monsoon winds were also extremely predictable. This made it particularly easy for the trader to plan trips according to their destination. For example, from October to November, cool, dry air is drawn from the Himalayas and blown southeast across the Arabian Sea towards Africa. This actually allowed merchants to make a round trip to India possibly within an year.
